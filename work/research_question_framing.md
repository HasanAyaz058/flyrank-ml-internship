# Research Question Framing

## Selected Lane
**Refresh / Content Opportunity Scoring (Core Lane)**

This project focuses on identifying content pages that should be prioritized for refresh or review based on observable search performance and content-related signals.

---

## Research Question

How can observable search and content signals be used to identify and prioritize web pages that are most likely to benefit from a content refresh?

---

## Decision

The decision is to determine which content pages should be reviewed first so that limited SEO and content resources are focused on pages with the highest potential for improvement.

---

## Unit of Analysis

The unit of analysis is a single content page.

---

## Output

The output will be a ranked list of content pages with a priority score indicating which pages should be reviewed or refreshed first.

---

## Action

An SEO or content manager can use the ranked list to decide which pages should be:

- Refreshed or updated
- Expanded with new information
- Monitored for future performance
- Reviewed manually before investing additional effort

---

## Cost of a Wrong Recommendation

A false positive may cause time and resources to be spent updating a page that does not require changes.

A false negative may leave an important declining page untreated, potentially reducing future search visibility and user engagement.

---

## Why Data and Machine Learning Help

The decision depends on multiple observable signals such as impressions, click-through rate (CTR), average position, content age, update history, and other measurable features.

Machine learning can combine these signals to recognize useful patterns that are difficult to capture with a simple manually written rule, helping prioritize pages more consistently while still allowing comparison with transparent baseline methods.

---

## Why This Is More Than "Train a Model"

The objective is not simply to train a machine learning model.

The project begins by defining a practical business problem, understanding the available data, identifying the risks of incorrect recommendations, selecting appropriate evaluation methods, and producing decision-support recommendations that someone can act upon.

---

## Scope and Cautious Language

This project aims to provide decision-support recommendations based on observed data patterns.

It does not claim to predict or explain search engine algorithms. Any recommendations should be treated as directional and validated before action is taken.
